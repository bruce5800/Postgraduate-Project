One problem, four markets

The same abstract structure — an offline side known in advance, an online side arriving one by one, and irrevocable matches — underlies very different real-world markets.

online side — arrives one by one offline side — known in advance bold edge — an irrevocable match
online advertising
offline:advertisers & ad slots
online:user impressions arriving
a match:show a compatible ad now
the canonical motivation — billions of impressions, decided in milliseconds
ride-hailing dispatch
offline:idle drivers nearby
online:ride requests arriving
a match:assign a driver to the rider
real-time and geographic — a wrong assignment can't be taken back
kidney / organ exchange
offline:available organs / donors
online:patients on the waitlist
a match:allocate to a compatible patient
strict compatibility constraints and ethical priority rules
job / task assignment
offline:workers / servers
online:jobs / tasks arriving
a match:route the task to a capable one
load balancing and scheduling — the streaming-systems angle
